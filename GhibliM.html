Ghibli Museum

The Ghibli Museum in Mitaka, Tokyo, designed by Hayao Miyazaki, is an enchanting, whimsical space dedicated to the art of animation. It features stained-glass windows, a rooftop garden with a Laputa robot, a cozy cinema (Saturn Theater) showing exclusive shorts, a Catbus playroom for children, and detailed, maze-like, immersive exhibitions.

More Information

Key Details for Visiting:
Operating Hours: 10:00 - 17:00 (often listed until 17:00 or 18:00, with strict closing times).
Closed Days: Tuesdays (if a national holiday falls on a Tuesday, the museum may be open).
Time Slots: Tickets have designated entry times: 10:00 a.m., 12:00 p.m., 2:00 p.m., and 4:00 p.m..
Ticketing: Tickets go on sale on the 10th of the month for the following month at 10:00 a.m. JST on the official Lawson Ticket website.
Official Ticket Prices (JPY):
Adults (19+): ¥1,000
Ages 13–18: ¥700
Ages 7–12: ¥400
Ages 4–6: ¥100
Ages 3 & Under: Free

Facilities

Key Facilities and Features:
Saturn Theater: A small cinema on the basement floor screening original, exclusive short films.
Permanent Exhibits (1st Floor): Dedicated to the history and techniques of animation, including a zoetrope with moving Ghibli character figures.
Temporary Special Exhibition (2nd Floor): Rotates periodically with specific themes or movies.
Rooftop Garden: Features a 5-meter-tall, moss-covered robot soldier from Castle in the Sky.
Straw Hat Café: A themed café offering light meals and drinks, including vegetarian options.
Mamma Aiuto! Gift Shop: Sells exclusive merchandise.
Children’s Play Area: Features a large, soft Catbus from My Neighbor Totoro.
Reading Room/Library: A small library with children's books and other materials.
Architecture & Interior: Designed by Hayao Miyazaki, the building features stained glass, spiral staircases, and hand-painted murals.
