Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle (Osaka-jo) is a premier, iconic landmark in central Osaka, Japan, built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi to symbolize power and unification. Reconstructed in 1931, the main tower features a museum, elevators, and an observation deck, surrounded by a massive park with moats, walls, and popular cherry blossoms.

More Information

Osaka Castle is generally open daily from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM (last entry 4:30 PM). The surrounding Osaka Castle Park is accessible 24/7.

Junior High Students & Younger Free Proof of age (passport for international visitors) may be required.

Facilities

Key Facilities at Osaka Castle
-Osaka Castle Main Tower (Museum): Floors 1–7 contain historical exhibits, including armor, weaponry, and AR displays. The 1st floor has a theater, and the 8th floor offers a 360-degree observation deck.
-Nishinomaru Garden: A large, paid-entry lawn garden with roughly 600 cherry trees, offering prime views of the castle tower.
-Accessibility: The main tower is equipped with elevators and is wheelchair accessible, with a dedicated elevator for the top floor available upon request.
-Souvenir Shops & Dining: Shops are located on the 1st and 8th floors of the museum. Several restaurants and kiosks are available in the surrounding park area.
-Samurai/Ninja Experience: Visitors can try on samurai armor or helmets for a fee and experience "Tate" (sword fighting techniques) at the nearby MIRAIZA Osaka-jo.
Osaka Castle Park Facilities
-Osaka-Jo Hall: A major 16,000-capacity arena for concerts and events.
-Sports Facilities: Includes a baseball field, archery range (Kyudō-Jō), and a martial arts training center (Shudokan).
-Other Attractions: The park also features the Hokoku Shrine, a plum grove with over 1,200 trees, and an open-air concert hall.
